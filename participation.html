Participant Worksheet

The Participant Worksheet obtains information on the total number and characteristics of students reached by initiatives designed to broaden participation in STEM. Specifically, the worksheet gathers information on the (1) number of students reached in a given year; (2) gender identity, race, and ethnicity of those students; and (3) number of first-generation college students (i.e., postsecondary students for whom neither parent completed a bachelorâ€™s degree or higher) reached. All of these data are collected in the aggregate, and no information about the characteristics of individual students are collected or displayed as part of this effort.

Several caveats regarding the use of the Participant Worksheet are worth noting:

  • The Participant Worksheet does not collect information about the types or frequency of activities that students participated in. Thus, for example, data cannot be used to distinguish between those students who participated in a 1-hour classroom activity and those who attended a 5-day educational summer program.
  • The data collected through the worksheet represents a duplicated count of studentsâ€”i.e., the number of times that students were reached (both within and across projects). Thus, for example, a student who participated in two separate activities in a given year would be counted twice.
  • The Participant Worksheet collects data on students reached directly and indirectly. Direct refers to students directly engaged in the STEM BP strategies conducted by a given project (e.g., students who knowingly participated in a defined activity designed to enhance their career awareness about STEM). Indirect refers to those students indirectly engaged by a project (e.g., students who benefited from a defined BP STEM education reform program, event, or activity without being aware of their engagement in that program, event, or activity).
  • For the purposes of the Participant Worksheet, school year is defined as September 1 to August 31. Projects may make changes to this timeline (e.g., including summer activities in the previous school year)â€”and these changes are documented in a caveats sheet and maintained throughout the duration of the collection.

Participant Worksheet

The Participant Worksheet obtains information on the total number and characteristics of educators reached by STEM BP initiatives. Specifically, the worksheet gathers information on (1) the number of educators reached in a given year; and (2) the gender identity, race, and ethnicity of those educators. All of these data are collected in the aggregate, and no information about the characteristics of individual educators are collected or displayed as part of this effort.

Several caveats regarding the use of the Participant Worksheet are worth noting:

  • The Participant Worksheet does not collect information about the types or frequency of activities that educators participated in. Thus, for example, data cannot be used to distinguish between those educators who participated in a 1-hour webinar and those who attended a 5-day professional development activity.
  • The data collected through the worksheet represents a duplicated count of educatorsâ€”i.e., the number of times that educators were reached (both within and across projects). Thus, for example, an educator who participated in two separate activities in a given year would be counted twice.
  • The Participant Worksheet collects data on educators reached directly and indirectly. Direct refers to educators directly engaged in the STEM BP strategies conducted by a given project (e.g., educators who knowingly participated in a defined activity designed to enhance the equity and quality of their instruction, mentoring, or advising). Indirect refers to those educators indirectly engaged by a project (e.g., educators who benefited from a defined BP STEM education reform program, event, or activity without being aware of their engagement in that program, event, or activity).
  • For the purposes of the Participant Worksheet, school year is defined as September 1 to August 31. Projects may make changes to this timeline (e.g., including summer activities in the previous school year)â€”and these changes are documented in a caveats sheet and maintained throughout the duration of the collection.
